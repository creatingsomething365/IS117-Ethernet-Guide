Matching Cables to Your Configuration

Choosing for the Home

A home network usually works fine with Cat5e for basic tasks like browsing and streaming. What matters is that it handles everyday browsing without fuss. However, for gamers, faster speeds come through Cat6, where lag fades because the cable carries more bandwidth.

Choosing for the Workplace

Faster needs at work usually mean Cat6 or even Cat6a makes more sense. Built to handle heavy demand and Power over Ethernet, Cat6a stands out when gear must run strong for many years. Where speed matters today plus stability tomorrow, this cable type fits without extra fuss.

"A good cable choice means solid speed that fits what you actually do."
